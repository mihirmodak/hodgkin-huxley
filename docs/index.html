A Computational Model of a Human Neuron

Mihir Modak • September 23, 2021 • MATLAB

Overview & Motivation

The Hodgkin-Huxley model is a mathematical model of the electrical activity of neurons, based on the movement of ions across the cell membrane through voltage-gated ion channels. Simulating this model using MATLAB involves solving a system of differential equations that describe the behavior of the ion channels in the neuron.

Tthe primary motivation for simulating neurons using the Hodgkin-Huxley model is to gain a better understanding of how individual ion channels can affect the overall activity of the neuron. It is able to consider a wide variety of ion conductances and kinetics to accurately simulate a neuron's behavior. It's also able to account for multiple simultaneous changes in the parameters and accurately reflect those changes in the neuron's behavior on the fly.

Challenges

THe Hodgkin-Huxley model involves multiple ion channels with varying conductances and kinetics. This complexity can make it challenging to figure out the appropriate parameters for a specific neuron or experimental condition. It's also difficult to choose the right initial conditions to start the model. If the parameters are set incorrectly, the neuron may show some unexpected behavior. It may never gain enough charge to fire (a dead neuron), or it may start firing quickly and erratically (kind of like a seizure).

This model can also be computationally expensive, since it's calculating the solution to a set of differential equations for every simulated time point. Optimizing the model parameters can be challenging due to the high number of parameters involved and the wide range of values that each parameter can take. The language used in this project (MATLAB) is already well-optimized, but the code still needed some further optimization to simulate the neuron for a long time.

Impact

Despite these challenges, simulating neurons using the Hodgkin-Huxley model has had a significant impact on our understanding of the nervous system. We were able to see the direct impact of sodium and potassium ions on the activity and behavior of the neuron. We were also able to simulate certain 'disease' states, such the rapid firing of seizures and even cell death due to toxicity.

The Hodgkin-Huxley model has helped researchers gain insights into a wide range of behaviors, from the generation of action potentials to the processing of sensory information in the brain. The model has also been used to develop controllers for epilepsy and epilespy-like conditions observed in neural prosthetics and brain-machine interfaces.
